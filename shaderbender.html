ShaderBender (2024)

An interactive body mirror experience

Inspired by Avatar: The Last Airbender, ShaderBender allows you to step into the role of an avatar with the ability to bend the elements. By mirroring your body on an avatar on-screen, ShaderBender invites you to explore how different ways of moving your body allows for the bending of different elements. Lifting your left hand might allow you to bend water, while lifting the right allows you to bend fire. Move your body and discover how you can become a true avatar.

If you would like to learn more about ShaderBender, you can find our website here.

My work on ShaderBender

Enviromental Effects

My main responsibility for ShaderBender involved setting the right atmoshpere for the experience. Early on we settled for a dojo in the mountains, with a thunderstorm in the background. To achieve this effect, I created two different particle systems: one for the rain, and one for the lightning. To create the dark clouds of a thunderstorm, while keeping the effect on performance low, I created a URP shader to simulate volumteric clouds. I also created the mountain in the background within Blender, vertex paint to achieve the desired snowy peak.

Sound Design and Code

As a secondary responsibility I worked on the sound design for ShaderBender. This mostly included sourcing sound effects for the elemental effects from royalty free sites, as well as mixing together a meditative sound track to play during the experience as a way to encourage mindful movement.
